Latest Project:

Numerical Study of High-Intensity Focused Ultrasound Ablation of Breast Tumour on an Anatomically Realistic Breast Phantom

Abstract:
High-intensity focused ultrasound (HIFU) ablation is a novel reliable technique with excellent potential for ablation and treating breast tumours. This method focuses high-intensity ultrasonic waves on the tumour to generate extreme heat and ablate cancerous tissues. The current study investigates a 3D physically realistic numerical simulation of HIFU ablation of breast tumours as a multi-physics problem. An anatomically realistic breast phantom (ARBP) is considered for the first time, and a bowl-shaped acoustic transducer is utilized to focus ultrasound waves at the tumour location. The acoustic physic is simulated, and the acoustic pressure field and sound intensity distribution are obtained. Then, bioheat transfer physic is simulated, and the amount of heat generated in the tumour area due to the applied focused high-intensity wave is calculated. In the end, the fraction of tumour tissue necrotized by this temperature rise is evaluated. The study’s results demonstrate that by applying 0.1 MPa pressure input pulse with 1.5 MHz frequency at the focal point, the acoustic pressure amplitude and sound intensity magnitude can reach a maximum of roughly 6E-06 Pa and 1.07E+07 Wm-2, respectively. The high sound intensity created at the focal point will elevate the temperature at this location to a maximum of roughly 80 °C after 10 s if the pressure input pulse is retained for 10 s. By continuing the ablation process without the acoustic source for another 90 s, the high temperature created in the tumour will cause necrosis of most of the tumour tissues. By numerically solving all of the various equation sets coupled together, this numerical platform can simulate the physics involved in the HIFU ablation of breast tumours with high accuracy. The results obtained from this 3D model can be much more accurate than previous 2D simulation models due to the consideration of sound diffraction in all three dimensions and various geometry details considered in the ARBP. Physicians can use this numerical platform as a reliable tool for predicting and measuring the approximate amount of temperature rise and the fraction of tumour tissues that will necrose, which can be highly valuable in determining the best HIFU therapy approach for various cases and patients.
